Features Impacting Power Use

Feeding power use data along with several time and environmental factors into an ML boosting model then into a SHAP explainer model reveals the impact of each feature.


SHAP values attempt to capture the effect of each feature independantly
Features with the greatest impact are listed top to buttom
Red dots = higher value for the features (higher temperature, later hours in the day)
Dots to the right = increases power use



Top 3 impactful features

  • Hour of day is the most important factor with 2200 MWh swing throughout the day (average hourly use = 5800 MWh)
  • Temperature. The warmest hours of the year can increase power use 3x the lowest use hours
  • Weekday vs Weekend is also very impactful with up to 1500 MWh throughout the week.

There's a few minor factors that also have some impact

  • Holidays shows significant power reduction, workplaces are closed, and people may be enjoying the outdoors
  • High humidity, daylight, and air pressure these factors make for hot, muggy days which may lead to indoor AC use, but most of this effect is captured by temperature
2 Time Cycles Drive Power Use

1. Daily power use tends to peak around 5pm and drops to a low around 3am
2. The weekly cycle is also very visible, with weekends (days 6-7) using significantly less power than weekdays (days 1-5). Also interesting is Sunday's power use cycle starts increasing later in the day vs Saturday. Perhaps people's day starts later on Sundays

The Other Major Impact Is Temperature

  • Power use lowest around 13 C and increase at both hotter and colder temperatures. Usage during hours of the hottest summer afternoons can triple the lowest midnight hours.
  • Highest hourly power use in the last 10 years was 10,285 MWh which occured on 2011-07-21 2pm with temperature at 37.2 C

    Simulated 2021 power usage at different temperatures
